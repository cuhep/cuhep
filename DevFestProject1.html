Food Insecurity

Food insecurity affects many New Yorkers and is a large stressor for more than a million New Yorkers.

According to Feeding America, the nation’s leading hunger-relief organization, 1 in 10 New Yorkers, or 1,882,580 people, struggle with hunger, and of them 596,060 are children. Nearly 1.2 million New Yorkers were food insecure even before the COVID-19 pandemic, with more than 185 million missing meals across the five boroughs, also known as “The Meal Gap.” Additionally, average meal costs in New York County are estimated to be $6.31, almost twice as high as the national average, $3.25.

New York Common Pantry

The New York Common Pantry picks up food from restaurants, possibly from overstocking food or to free up space. The good thing about this program is it allows for food not to be wasted by giving extra food to those in need. It's a win-win situation for everyone.
