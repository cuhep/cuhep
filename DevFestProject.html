With Care, Your Neighbor Project Mission

Homelessness adds an indescribable amount of stress onto the human psyche. Often, some of the needs people struggle to fulfill are access to food, a safe space to spend the night, access to medical attention, among other things. The fear of lack of safety, food, and New York City's harsh weather conditions are constant stressors which can take a toll on the human psyche. The effect of homelessness on one's mental health is a problem, especially as many people "fall off the grid" and don't receive the mental health treatment they need. Let's aim to close this gap, and help each other as New Yorkers. This project aims to:
1. Bring access to resources to one place
2. Connect people in need with professionals or trained volunteers in their community who can help
3. Giving alternative support and resources which are accessible to all New Yorkers
4. Partner with LinkNYC to make these resources readily available across the City

Resources and Hotline Quick Links

Hotlines:
Crisis Text Line: New York State has partnered with Crisis Text Line, an anonymous texting service available 24/7. Starting a conversation is easy. Text GOT5 to 741741.

OASAS HOPEline: New York State’s 24/7 problem gambling and chemical dependency hotline. For Help and Hope call 1-877-8-HOPENY or text HOPENY

988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline: If your life or someone else's is in imminent danger, please call 911. If you are in crisis and need immediate help, please call: 988

Domestic Violence: If you or someone else is in a relationship is being controlled by another individual through verbal, physical, or sexual abuse, or other tactics, please call: 1-800-942-6906

NYC WELL: New York City’s free, confidential support and crisis intervention for anyone seeking help for mental health and/or substance misuse concerns, available 24/7. Text “WELL” to 65173 or call 1-888-NYC-WELL.
